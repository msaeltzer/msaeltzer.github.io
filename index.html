Welcome to my homepage! I am a doctoral researcher in political science focusing on comparitive politics. My research revolves around the dimensions of conflict politics revolves around. What issues matter for the public, political parties and their constituencies? To answer these questions I apply methods of quantatitive text analysis to all kinds of political text. I am particularly interested in internal conflicts inside political parties. This leads me to studying social media data, in particular Twitter. Politicians can take positions beyond the party line, so we can observe differences between members of the same party we would typically miss. I answer these questions using text-as-data. My dissertation develops means to summarize and compare positions of parties and individual politicians on a common scale.

Positions and Polarization

What constitutes political competition, what is it about and how does it develop. I work on the dimensioanality of political space and how individual politicians and parties take positions. I analyze social media data, parliamentary speeches and manifestos, but also work with surveys.

Social Media

Social media allows us to observe correlates of political behavior, it also might influence politics itself through fake news, algorithms and feedback mechanisms. The effect of social media on political elites is underresearched and part of my research agenda.

Methods

Considering myself as much a "political engineer" as a political scientist, I have a strong focu on the application of modern data driven approaches to politics. We live in the age of infinite data available to research and I work to utilize for our understanding of traditional questions of political science as much as new questions that arise particularly from it.

Text as Data

Political science has a long tradition in the analysis of text: speeches, manifestos or other sources. With the emergence of computational capabilities, it developed new ways to analyze the vast amounts of new text created in political context every day. My research agenda heavily focuses on the automated analysis of this text.

Computational

Modern challenges to text analysis and the sheer existence of social media necessitate the ability to proces this data, both in terms of personal skills like programming and understanding of computation, but also the ability to develop and maintain the respecitve infrastructure. One of the core features of replicable research with large amounts of text or networks is the ability to store and process them.

Data Collection

Social sciences are heading for a revolution in the wide availability of data in the internet. By using sources from databases, documents on servers, social media or webpages like wikipedia or political organizations. To collect them, it is necessary to develop tools for data Collection from web sources. I have applied and taught methods of collecting and handling web data.

List of Projects

Birdfish
At the core of my dissertation, I developed a way to scale Twitter text into ideological positions

2020 U.S. Election Database
Developed as a course project, students collected Twitterdata, encyclopedic information and campaign finance data for the upcoming election in the United States. Using web scraping, APIs and hand coding, more than 2200 candidates from the primary process onwards are linked to their Twitter profiles and campaign records.

InterpellaR
As part of the DFG funded project "Populist Challenge in Parliaments" we scrape, download, OCR and parse more than 300,000 parliamentary questions in 15 German federal states and link them to a relational database.

StateLegislatoR
As part of the DFG funded project "Populist Challenge in Parliaments" we create a relational database of state legislators after the role model of Simon Munzert's and Sascha Göbel's legislatoR. Like legislatoR, it is based on wikipedia.
